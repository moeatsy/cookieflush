Behavior

When CookieFlush should clean cookies for sites you haven't whitelisted.

How long CookieFlush waits after a tab closes before sweeping. While “Also remove third-party cookies” is on (the default), the sweep clears all cookies for sites with no open tab — first- and third-party — so this grace period covers the site you just closed too. Reopen a tab within the window and its cookies are kept; bulk tab closes group into one sweep. With third-party removal off, the closed site is cleaned immediately and this delay doesn’t apply.

Storage to clean

What else gets removed alongside cookies. Requires an extra Chrome permission — granted just-in-time the first time you turn one on.

Whitelist (always keep cookies)

Sites you want to stay signed in to. Subdomains are protected automatically — whitelisting google.com also protects mail.google.com. Pick This host only to protect exactly the address you type instead.

    Greylist (temporary)

    Sites protected for a fixed window. Add greylist entries by clicking the toolbar icon on the site you want to protect.

      Cookie rules (advanced)

      Fine-grained control over individual cookies by name, instead of all-or-nothing per site. Most people never need this — leave it off and the whitelist works as before.

      Quick presets (all sites)

      For cookies whose names are the same across thousands of sites. One click adds them to the global rules below.

      Rules

      Scheduled sweep

      Periodically clear cookies for sites that have no open tabs and aren't whitelisted. Closed-and-forgotten sites get cleaned even if you never close their original tab.

      Activity

      Local log of cleanups. Stored on this device only — never synced or sent anywhere.

        Sync & backup

        Whitelist, greylist, and settings sync across your Chrome devices via Chrome's own storage.sync. Logs and stats stay local.
